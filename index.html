Slide 04 · Setback & recovery
The time the plan didn't work
NRR trajectory at Reward Gateway · Early expansion motion failed · What we changed
Reward Gateway · 2011–2016
NRR index · Entry = 100 (representing 65% NRR baseline) · Recovery trajectory
↓ Month 8 — Initial expansion motion launched. NRR deteriorated further. Wrong ICP for upsell, wrong trigger point, wrong message.
↑ Month 14 — Redesigned activation model. Shifted from volume expansion to value expansion. NRR inflected.
What I learned
The first expansion motion I designed made things worse. It was too aggressive, too early, to the wrong segment. Rebuilding from that taught me more about retention economics than anything else in my career.

The three things I changed
  • Killed the volume-based expansion model and rebuilt around value expansion — only upsell when the customer has genuinely activated and seen ROI
  • Changed the trigger for expansion conversations from "contract renewal date" to "customer success milestone" — this alone moved NRR significantly
  • Reduced attrition from 50% to sub-10% by addressing the underlying product adoption problem, not the churn problem. If activation works, retention follows.
